## H1: "Stripe/PayPal 같은 전통 금융이 A2A 경제를 못 따라잡진 않겠죠?"

### [FACT] Evidence Collected
1. **Stripe Tempo blockchain** launched testnet (Feb 2026) with Visa, Nubank, Shopify, Klarna
2. **Stripe acquired Bridge** for stablecoin infrastructure integration
3. **PayPal PYUSD** expanded to 70 markets (Mar 2026)
4. **Stripe-PayPal acquisition talks** ongoing (Bloomberg, Feb 2026)
5. **x402 protocol data:** USDC = 98.6% of agent payment settlements currently
6. **MPP (Machine Payment Protocol):** Co-authored by Stripe & Tempo (Mar 18, 2026)

### [THESIS] Analysis

**Opportunity Scanner (Bull):**
Traditional fintech CAN serve A2A, but they're choosing stablecoins as the settlement layer. Stripe's Tempo uses stablecoins. PayPal has PYUSD. The question isn't "stablecoins vs traditional rails"—it's "whose stablecoin wins."

**Sector Skeptic (Critic):**
"Circle is middleware that gets squeezed." If Stripe/PayPal control the checkout layer (ACP, MPP) and the settlement layer (Tempo, Bridge, PYUSD), they capture both ends. USDC becomes commoditized plumbing—necessary but low-margin.

**Cross-Domain Analogy (SWIFT/Visa):**
SWIFT survived because no bank wanted competitors controlling settlement. But SWIFT is a consortium, not a profit-center. Visa early days: first-mover advantage in network effects, but regulatory moat mattered more. Circle's regulatory moat is real—but Tether just hired Big Four. PYUSD is Paxos-issued (regulated). The regulatory moat is eroding.

### H1 Verdict
**⚠️ MIXED** — Traditional fintech is NOT building alternatives to stablecoins; they're building ON stablecoins. But they don't need USDC specifically. Stripe has Bridge. PayPal has PYUSD. Circle's moat is: (1) first-mover, (2) regulatory, (3) CCTP cross-chain infrastructure. #1 is fading. #2 is eroding. #3 is strong but monetization unclear.

**Edge Test:** Can Shawn articulate why USDC wins over PYUSD/USDT when both get regulated? Answer: USDC's cross-chain interoperability (CCTP) and DeFi integration depth. But this is a 🟡 Moderate edge, not 🟢 Strong.

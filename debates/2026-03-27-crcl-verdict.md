### Divergence #3: Velocity vs. Volume (The A2A Paradox)

| Bull | Bear |
|------|------|
| Volume growth offsets rate compression; if circulation doubles, revenue grows even with lower rates | Velocity HURTS yield — faster A2A settlement = less idle balance earning interest |

**Judge's Assessment:** This is the most intellectually interesting disagreement. 

**The Bear is technically correct:** If USDC is used for instant A2A payments (the bull case for adoption), the average *duration* each dollar sits in Circle's reserve shrinks. Example:
- $1M sitting idle for 30 days = ~$3,500 yield
- $1M moving 4x per month (A2A velocity) = average $250K idle = ~$875 yield

**BUT the Bull has a valid counter:** If A2A expands USDC's *total addressable volume*, the net effect could still be positive. More users × smaller balances could still = higher total reserves on average.

**Edge: Slight Edge to Bear** — this is an underappreciated structural risk that deserves more weight.
